# Fleet Copilot — GTM point of view (optional deliverable)

## Target customer

**For-hire fleets running 5–50 trucks** — roughly 50k US carriers. The buyer
and the daily user are usually the same two people: an owner who feels cost
per mile and cash flow, and a dispatcher wearing four hats (dispatch, track,
comply, bill). They either run no TMS at all — spreadsheets, phones, a
QuickBooks export — or a legacy TMS they actively dislike.

Why this segment first:
- Pain density is highest: one person absorbs every one of the five problem
  areas daily, so a single product that collapses them lands hard.
- No procurement committee; decisions close in days on visible ROI.
- Trucker Path already has distribution into exactly these fleets.

## Wedge and sequencing

**Land with the Billing Auditor.** It is the only module that finds *money*
in week one: unclaimed detention documented by POD dock times and GPS dwell,
missed accessorials, under-billed rate confirmations. "We found $412 on your
last 12 loads" is a sales pitch a fleet owner can audit themselves — every
finding links to the document and the math.

Then expand in the order trust is earned:
1. **Billing Audit** (wedge — provable found money, days-to-invoice drops to
   same-day)
2. **Proactive Alerts + Smart Dispatch** once telemetry is native (TP ELD /
   Command integration) — the daily-habit surface
3. **Cost + Safety** — the retention moat: 3 years of the fleet's own history
   becomes the benchmark they can't leave behind

## Pricing

- **Billing Audit**: $30–40 / truck / month. Anchors against one recovered
  detention event (~$140) paying for a truck-month.
- **Full Copilot**: $75–90 / truck / month once the fleet's approval-rate and
  edit-rate metrics clear the trust bar. A 30-truck fleet lands ≈ $32k ACV —
  under the ~$120/truck/month all-in that fleets already pay for ELD + TMS
  stacks, while replacing several point tools.
- Per-truck pricing (not per-seat): fleets grow trucks, not dispatchers, and
  the value scales with loads monitored, not logins.

## Acquisition

- **The driver network is the funnel.** 1M+ drivers already run Trucker Path
  in the cab; drivers of small fleets are the ones being chased for
  paperwork. Driver-side document scans seed the fleet-side audit — the
  product acquires its own buyer ("your driver already scanned 14 BOLs this
  month — see what your invoices missed").
- **Install-base upsell**: TP ELD / Command fleets get the copilot as a tier
  upgrade — telemetry is already flowing, activation is one click. CAC near
  zero versus cold outbound.
- **Proof-first motion**: free 14-day "billing archaeology" on the fleet's
  last 90 days of paperwork; convert on found dollars.

## What I'd watch

- Activation: time-to-first-approved-action (< 15 min from install)
- P&L: deadhead % of miles, days-to-invoice, detention recovered / 100 loads,
  exceptions caught before the customer called
- Trust: approval rate (60–95% healthy band) and edit-before-approve rate
  trending down — the gate for raising the autonomy ladder
